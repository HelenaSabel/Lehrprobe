Practise

Practical application of the acquired knowledge

The task consists in presenting a digital edition project that involves this facsimile: a diary paper by Emily Brontë (26 June 1837) that includes a sketch of herself and her sister Anne working.

Description of the task:

  1. Establish the context of the editing project that must include the edition of this paper. There is metadata in the correspondant entry of the British Library, but you must define the context of the project which needs to answer the question “Why am I editing these materials in this particular way?” For this exercise, the answer to this question can be completely fictitious, since your specialisation might not be related to 19th century England. The editing project could include the edition of other works (e.g. the letters by the Brontë sisters), and it must include a research question.
  2. Define the main elements of the editing model according to the objectives you have defined in the previous step. To help you in this task, you can evaluate the pertinence of the following questions (some of them inspired by the MLA Guidelines for Editors of Scholarly Editions, others by Pierazzo 2015):
    • Which are the scholarly reasons for which a particular edition of this work or text is necessary?
    • What agents have participated in the production of the object being edited?
    • Is it important to reproduce the material sources in facsimile as part of the edition?
    • Which are the extension and the relevance of the non-verbal content?
    • Are there more than one witness? What is the importance of multiple versions and multiple states?
    • According to the purpose of the edition, what are the levels of transcription that I want to implement? (see Driscoll 2006; online version)
    • What kind of annotations should the edition have?
    • What are the functionalities of the interface? What is the role of interactive tools in this edition?
