Rationale

A new research paradigm

  • Large collections of electronic resources enable
    • new research questions
    • new methodologies
      • corpus based and corpus driven research
      • working through the noise (e.g. Franzini et al. 2018)

Efficient workflows

Challenges when reusing existing datasets

  • Text selection: cultural and historical significance is different accross domains (and research questions)
    • Metadata contained in the source meaningful for the selection
      • via formalised content
      • via the use of IDs from authority records that enable an automatic extraction of metadata from other sources (RDF endpoints)

Challenges when reusing existing datasets

  • Hetereogenous editorial criteria
    • Sources with no descriptive markup and heterogeneity within a standard
    • Editorial pseudo-markup and coexistance of different graphical conventions
    • Different orthographic conventions
    • Abbreviations
    • Different ecdotic typologies

Challenges when reusing existing datasets

  • Solution: creation of “editorial profiles” as a means of homogenisation

Challenges when reusing existing datasets

  • Philological exactness
    • Solution: automatic collation of the open source text with a digitised reference edition when the latter is under copyright

Challenges when reusing existing datasets

  • Dependency between the dataset and the publication/exploitation platform
    • Solution: conversion of platform-specific formats to TEI

Machine-assisted pipelines

  • From images to annotated, machine-readable texts
    • Pre-trained models and easy-to-use training algorithms
    • From transcription file formats to TEI through
      • scripting (e.g. DiScholEd pipeline, LECTAUREP - Page2tei)
      • machine-learning approaches (e.g. Khemakhem et al. 2017, Pagel et al. 2021, Ruiz et al. 2022)

Machine-assisted pipelines

  • Automatic collation
    • Hypercollate, processing of TEI-XML transcriptions of texts with variation (Bleeker et al. 2022)

Machine-assisted pipelines

  • Linguistic and literary annotations through Natural Language Processing (NLP)
    • Challenges:
      • Lower performance for historical varieties and low-resource languages
      • Combination of marked-up and plain text formats during the annotation pipeline
        • To cite a few exceptions: NEISS TEI Entity Enricher (Lemke & Sperfeld 2023), acdh-spacytei (Andorfer & Schlögl 2021), Standoff Tools (Lück 2023)

Machine-assisted pipelines

  • Machine-assisted manual annotation through active learning
    • Example: INCEpTION (Klie et al. 2018)

Interface sustainability

  • Issues: assuring the infrastructure and maintenance after the funding period, deprecation of dependencies, changes in browser support, etc.
  • (Partial) solution: Out-of-the-box corpus exploitation platforms
    • The role of Virtual Research Environments in digital textual criticism (Tóth-Czifra 2021). Examples:
      • eLaborate, eScriptorium, FairCopy, TextGrid, Textual Communities
    • Existence of several resources with specific functionalities that enable text and image visualisation, queries, and/or statistical analyses. Examples:
      • Publication: TEI Publisher, Ediarium, EVT
      • Corpus exploitation: CorpusExplorer, eDictor, PhiloLogic, TEITOK, TXM

If custom interface: Its preservation as a cultural object

  • Rationale: interface as part of the philological discourse (Andrews & van Zundert 2018)
  • Presentational information (including interface functionalities) as part of the data model
    • TEI Processing Model (TEI Consortium 2023)
    • Relation with the bibliographical study of digital texts and artifacts (e.g. ebooks, videogames, digital recordings of artistic performances)

From open data to open science

Data usability

  • TEI and linked data
    • Relation between TEI-XML and graph-based technologies (e.g. Bleeker, Dekker et al. 2022, Eide 2015)
    • Creation of linked data from TEI resources (e.g. Crompton & Schwartz 2018, Eide & Ore 2007, Page et al. 2019)
    • New ecdotic models (e.g. the assertive edition: Vogeler 2019)
    • Out-of-the-box tools: LEAF-Writer

Conclusions

Final remarks

  • Quality and quantity are not trade-offs thanks to:
    • Quality assessment of machine-assisted pipelines (e.g. Bläß et al. 2022)
  • The role of the editor:
    • Designing the pipeline, selecting the tools and automatic annotation models closest to project needs
    • Establishing the acceptable margin of error
    • Prioritising human correction needs
